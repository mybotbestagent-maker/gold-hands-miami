Interior Painting Cost in Miami: What to Expect in 2026

May 18, 20269 min readPainting

Thinking about repainting your home? Smart move. A fresh coat of paint is one of the fastest and most affordable ways to transform a space. But how much should you actually expect to pay in Miami in 2026? This guide breaks it down room by room, with real numbers based on current local market rates.

Average Interior Painting Costs in Miami (2026)

Here is what you can expect to pay for professional interior painting in the Miami area:

  • Accent wall (single wall): $250 - $400
  • Standard bedroom (10x12): $400 - $700
  • Large bedroom or living room (12x15+): $600 - $1,000
  • Bathroom: $300 - $500
  • Kitchen: $500 - $900
  • 1-bedroom apartment/condo: $1,200 - $2,000
  • 2-bedroom apartment/condo: $1,800 - $3,000
  • 3-bedroom house: $3,000 - $5,500

These prices typically include labor, prep work (taping, covering furniture and floors), two coats of paint, and cleanup. Paint itself may or may not be included — always ask.

What Affects the Price?

1. Room Size and Ceiling Height

Painters price by square footage of wall area, not floor area. A 10x12 room with 8-foot ceilings has about 350 square feet of wall space. Higher ceilings (common in newer Miami condos) mean more wall area and higher costs. The typical rate in Miami is $2.50 to $4.50 per square foot of wall area.

2. Number of Coats

Two coats is standard for a quality finish. If you are changing from a dark color to a light one, you may need a primer coat plus two finish coats — that is three coats total, which adds 30-40% to the cost.

3. Paint Quality

There is a real difference between budget paint and premium paint. Here is what to expect:

  • Builder-grade paint: $25-$35 per gallon. Works, but may need more coats and does not hold up as well over time.
  • Mid-range (Sherwin-Williams ProMar, Behr Ultra): $40-$55 per gallon. Good coverage, durable, and the sweet spot for most homeowners.
  • Premium (Benjamin Moore Regal, Sherwin-Williams Emerald): $60-$85 per gallon. Excellent coverage in fewer coats, stain-resistant, washable. Worth it for high-traffic areas.

In Miami's humid climate, we recommend at minimum a mid-range paint with mildew resistance. Cheap paint in South Florida is a false economy — you will repaint sooner.

4. Prep Work Required

This is where costs can vary significantly. Clean, smooth walls in good condition require minimal prep. But walls with holes, cracks, old wallpaper residue, or peeling paint need extra attention. Extensive prep can add 20-50% to the total cost — but skipping it means a finish that looks bad and does not last.

5. Trim, Baseboards, and Doors

Wall painting quotes often do not include trim work. Painting baseboards, door frames, window frames, and crown molding is usually an add-on priced per room ($150-$300 per room) or per linear foot. If your trim is outdated or a different color than you want, factor this in.

6. Ceiling Painting

Ceilings are typically quoted separately. Expect to add $150-$350 per room for ceiling painting. A flat or matte finish is standard for ceilings as it hides imperfections.

How to Avoid Overpaying

  1. Get at least three quotes. Compare prices, but also compare what is included. The cheapest quote often leaves out prep work or uses builder-grade paint.
  2. Ask what is included. Does the quote include paint? Primer? Prep? Trim? Moving furniture? Cleanup? Get it in writing.
  3. Check the painter's insurance. An uninsured painter who damages your floors or furniture becomes your problem.
  4. Do not pay 100% upfront. A reasonable deposit is 25-30%. Pay the balance upon satisfactory completion.
  5. Be flexible on scheduling. Some painters offer discounts for weekday work or if they can fit you in between larger jobs.

DIY vs. Hiring a Pro

Can you paint a room yourself? Absolutely. Should you? It depends. Painting a small bedroom is a manageable weekend project. But painting multiple rooms, high ceilings, or complex spaces with trim work is where professionals earn their fee. A pro crew can paint a room in 4-6 hours that would take a homeowner an entire weekend.

The real value of professional painting is in the prep work and the finish quality. Clean tape lines, even coverage, no drips, proper drying between coats — these details separate a professional result from a DIY one.

The Bottom Line

Interior painting in Miami in 2026 costs anywhere from $2.50 to $4.50 per square foot of wall area, depending on the factors above. For an average room, budget $400-$800. For a full apartment or house, $1,500-$5,000+. Get multiple quotes, ask the right questions, and prioritize quality paint and prep work over the lowest price.
